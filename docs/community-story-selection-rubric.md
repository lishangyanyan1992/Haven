# Community Story Selection Rubric

Use this rubric when deciding which outside-source community stories are worth collecting, importing, rewriting, staging, or publishing for Haven.

This document does not replace `docs/outside-source-story-policy.md`. The policy controls source selection, privacy, import mechanics, admin review, and stats separation. This rubric controls story fit: whether a candidate story is actually useful for Haven's value proposition, target audience, and business goals.

If the user asks to pull new stories and has specified a source, apply this rubric before import. If the user has not specified a source, stop and ask which source to use.

## Strategic Goal

Haven should collect stories that help global talent make practical U.S. immigration decisions during stressful, high-stakes moments.

The best stories make a user think:

- "This person is in a situation like mine."
- "I understand what they did next."
- "I can compare my timeline, risks, and options."
- "Haven understands the specific immigration moment I am in."

Do not optimize for story volume. Optimize for relevance, specificity, decision value, and trust.

## Current 30-Day Priority

For the current operating period, prioritize:

1. H-1B layoffs
2. Sponsor search
3. Stories that improve signup conversion, AI expert quality, and SEO

Treat green card timing, OPT transitions, and broader AI expert coverage as secondary unless they directly support H-1B layoff or sponsor-search decisions.

Do not pull a story simply because it is immigration-related. It must support the current priority or fill a clear gap in the H-1B layoff/sponsor-search journey.

## Target Audience

Prioritize stories from or about:

- F-1, OPT, and STEM OPT students
- H-1B workers
- H-1B workers facing layoffs, job changes, or transfer timing
- People in or approaching the 60-day grace period
- Employment-based green card applicants
- People tracking PERM, I-140, I-485, EB-1, EB-2, EB-3, or priority dates
- People affected by visa bulletin movement, retrogression, or backlog uncertainty
- Spouses and dependents affected by H-4, H-4 EAD, F-2, or related status decisions
- Workers trying to identify realistic sponsor employers

The strongest current wedge is H-1B workers dealing with layoffs, transfers, sponsor search, and green card uncertainty.

## Story Fit Tiers

### Tier 1: Core Haven Stories

Pull these first.

These stories map directly to Haven's strongest product surfaces: layoff planning, H-1B transfer support, sponsor search, timeline tracking, green card planning, and AI expert answers.

Examples:

- H-1B layoff with last working day, grace-period timing, and next steps
- H-1B transfer after layoff or job change
- H-1B transfer with premium processing, RFE, NOID, approval, denial, or withdrawal
- H-1B to B-2, H-4, F-1, Day 1 CPT, O-1, or cap-exempt backup path
- OPT or STEM OPT worker moving to H-1B or facing status gap risk
- I-140 approved worker evaluating job change or layoff risk
- PERM/I-140/I-485 timing affected by employer change
- Priority date, visa bulletin, retrogression, or adjustment filing timing issue
- H-4/H-4 EAD spouse impact during layoff, transfer, or green card delay
- Sponsor-search story with concrete employer, recruiter, LCA, or sponsorship timing lessons

### Tier 2: Useful Adjacent Stories

Pull selectively when they add clear decision value or fill a content gap.

Examples:

- F-1 CPT/OPT compliance problems tied to future H-1B or green card risk
- RFEs or denials with transferable lessons for employment-based applicants
- Document preparation, evidence organization, or attorney communication stories
- Immigration medical, vaccine, or I-693 issues when tied to green card filing timing
- Consular stamping or travel stories when tied to H-1B, F-1, or employment-based status
- Lawyer selection or legal-fee stories when tied to a concrete work-visa or green-card decision

### Tier 3: Low Priority Or Off-Strategy

Skip by default unless the user explicitly asks to expand into the category. During the current operating period, fully exclude family-based, asylum, citizenship, and humanitarian stories.

Examples:

- Generic immigration anxiety without specific facts
- Broad political debate or policy venting
- Naturalization/citizenship stories not tied to an existing Haven product surface
- Family or marriage green card stories not tied to employment-based strategy
- Asylum, humanitarian, removal, or detention stories unless Haven intentionally expands there
- Tourist visa, K-1, DV lottery, or investor stories unless tied to a current campaign
- Pure legal advice posts with no personal timeline, action, or outcome
- News commentary without a personal case experience

## Scoring Rubric

Score each candidate from 0 to 20. Prefer importing stories scoring 16 or higher. Consider 13 to 15 only when they fill a known gap. Skip below 13.

During the current operating period, a story should also match H-1B layoff or sponsor-search priorities. If it does not, skip it even if the general score is high.

This score is for review prioritization, not automatic exclusion. Unless a story violates the outside-source policy, privacy rules, freshness rules, or current hard exclusions, keep borderline candidates in the review notes instead of silently discarding them.

### 1. Audience Fit: 0-4

- 4: Directly matches H-1B, OPT/STEM OPT, employment-based green card, layoff, transfer, or priority-date audience.
- 3: Related to work/student status or dependent status with clear overlap.
- 2: General U.S. immigration story with some relevance.
- 1: Weak overlap with current Haven audience.
- 0: Not relevant to Haven's current audience.

### 2. Decision Value: 0-4

- 4: Shows a concrete decision, tradeoff, action, or outcome another user can learn from.
- 3: Has useful practical steps but outcome is incomplete.
- 2: Has some context but limited actionable value.
- 1: Mostly emotional or speculative.
- 0: No meaningful decision value.

### 3. Case Specificity: 0-4

- 4: Includes status, timing, action, and result or current state.
- 3: Includes most key facts but one important piece is missing.
- 2: Includes a status and general question but little timeline.
- 1: Very vague.
- 0: Cannot extract useful case context.

### 4. Product Alignment: 0-4

- 4: Supports an existing Haven surface such as layoff planner, advisor, sponsor jobs, priority-date checker, timeline, community matching, or document prep.
- 3: Supports a likely near-term surface or resource page.
- 2: Useful education but not connected to a product workflow.
- 1: Interesting but not product-relevant.
- 0: No clear product connection.

### 5. Safe Rewrite Potential: 0-4

- 4: Easy to anonymize while preserving the useful facts.
- 3: Some identifiers need removal but story remains useful.
- 2: Heavy generalization required.
- 1: High privacy or similarity risk.
- 0: Should not be imported.

## Prioritization Signal Score

After the base 20-point score, assign a prioritization signal score from 0 to 20. This helps rank stories for review without excluding too many candidates.

Give each signal 0, 1, or 2 points:

- Clear before/after: The story has a recognizable situation, action, and result or current state.
- Decision fork: The person faced a meaningful choice, such as transfer vs B-2, H-4 vs F-1, Day 1 CPT vs leaving, or sponsor search vs non-sponsored work.
- Specific timeline: The story includes dates, day counts, filing windows, last working day, grace-period timing, premium-processing timing, or other time anchors.
- Transferable lesson: Another Haven user could reuse the lesson, checklist item, warning, or decision frame.
- Search demand: The story matches likely search queries such as "H1B layoff 60 days", "H1B transfer after layoff", "H1B to B2", "I-140 approved laid off", or "H1B sponsor search".
- Comment quality: Comments add practical corrections, options, timelines, real-world warnings, prior experiences, or expertise signals.
- Misconception correction: The story clarifies a common misunderstanding, such as grace-period start date, LCA vs sponsorship, I-140 portability, premium processing limits, or unemployment-day rules.
- Outcome diversity: The story adds a useful non-duplicate outcome type to the batch, such as approval, RFE, denial, delay, withdrawal, pending after filing, sponsor found, or status change.
- Trust/safety: The story is credible enough to rewrite, not sensational, not overly employer-name-heavy, and not dependent on unverifiable claims about a named person.
- Narrative clarity: A reader can understand the rewritten story in under 30 seconds.

Signal scoring:

- 2: Strong signal
- 1: Partial signal
- 0: Missing or weak signal

Recommended review priority:

- 32-40 combined score: High priority
- 24-31 combined score: Reviewable
- 18-23 combined score: Borderline, include in notes if recent and on-priority
- Below 18 combined score: Usually skip unless it fills a specific requested gap

Combined score = base score plus prioritization signal score.

Do not use the signal score as the only selection mechanism. A story with a lower signal score may still be valuable if it fills a scarce segment or source gap.

## Meaningful Comment Value Score

Assign a separate meaningful comment value score from 0 to 10. This score should carry significant weight because high-quality comments often contain the practical community knowledge Haven wants to preserve.

Meaningful comments include:

- Real suggestions: concrete next steps, options, checklists, timing advice, attorney/process questions to ask, or recruiter/employer tactics.
- Previous experiences: commenters sharing what happened in a similar H-1B layoff, transfer, B-2 bridge, LCA delay, RFE/NOID, sponsor-search, or green-card-stage situation.
- Proven expertise signals: comments from immigration attorneys, experienced filers, repeat contributors, people citing specific USCIS process mechanics, or comments that carefully distinguish what they know from what they do not know.

Score comments this way:

- 9-10: Multiple high-signal comments with concrete suggestions, similar prior experiences, and credible expertise/process detail.
- 7-8: At least one strong comment with specific prior experience or concrete next-step guidance.
- 4-6: Some useful comments, but incomplete, generic, conflicting, or lightly supported.
- 1-3: Mostly sympathy, speculation, jokes, anxiety, or low-detail advice.
- 0: No meaningful comments available.

Comments should improve review priority, but lack of comments should not automatically disqualify a fresh, high-fit story. For emerging stories posted in the last 24 to 48 hours, keep promising low-comment candidates in the review notes because comments may accumulate later.

Final review score = base score + prioritization signal score + meaningful comment value score.

Recommended final review priority:

- 40-50: High priority
- 35-39: Reviewable
- 30-34: Borderline, include in notes only if recent and on-priority
- Below 30: Usually skip unless it fills a specific requested gap

## Minimum Required Fields

Do not import a candidate unless it has:

- Source name
- Stable `source_story_id`
- Source URL or source locator
- Original language, if not English
- Title or short summary
- Body or source text
- Immigration status or category, if available
- Timeline/action/outcome fields, if available
- Comments only when they add practical value

If the story lacks enough immigration facts to classify, skip it.

## Preferred Story Attributes

Prioritize stories with:

- A clear moment of urgency
- A practical next step
- A real constraint, deadline, or tradeoff
- A before/after timeline
- A user-visible outcome, even if pending
- Comments that correct misconceptions or add useful options
- Details that improve matching by visa status, country, category, employer stage, or green card stage

Prioritize stories with outcomes over unresolved questions. Outcome can mean approved, denied, RFE/NOID received, transfer filed, sponsor found, status changed, departed, or still pending after a clear action.

Unresolved questions may be pulled only when they expose a high-value H-1B layoff or sponsor-search pain point and include enough facts for Haven's AI expert to answer better. Even then, the story must include at least one concrete recommendation, idea, or proposed approach from the poster or a commenter. Do not pull stories that only pose an open-ended question with no ideas, suggestions, or proposed path forward.

## SEO Rewrite Requirements

When rewriting selected stories and comments for public community review, optimize the public draft for SEO without making it read like marketing copy.

The rewritten post should:

- Use a clear long-tail title that includes the visa status, problem, and decision point when accurate.
- Include natural search phrases in the title and body, such as H-1B layoff, 60-day grace period, H-1B transfer, B-2 bridge, sponsor search, STEM OPT, LCA timing, I-140, PERM, priority date, or other terms directly supported by the source.
- Preserve the lived-experience forum tone and first-person perspective.
- Keep comments useful for search by retaining practical terms, warnings, timelines, and process names when accurate.
- Avoid keyword stuffing, generic SEO filler, employer names, unsupported legal claims, or adding facts that were not in the source.
- Prefer specific decision language over broad labels, such as "H-1B transfer while B-2 is pending after layoff" instead of only "H-1B question."

## Freshness Rules

Use recent stories only.

- Always look at the freshest available stories first.
- Rank newer qualifying stories above older qualifying stories when relevance and safety are comparable.
- Review candidates in reverse chronological order before broadening queries or accepting older stories.
- Prefer stories from the last 15 days.
- Accept stories from the last 30 days when there are not enough strong 15-day candidates.
- Do not pull older stories unless the user explicitly asks for an evergreen or historical batch.
- If no suitable recent stories are available for the specified source and priority, do not broaden silently. Report that no qualifying stories were available.

When source dates are ambiguous, use the best available timestamp from the source page or collector metadata. If freshness cannot be established, skip the story unless the user explicitly approves uncertain-date candidates.

## Pull Mix Guidance

For a batch of 10 outside-source stories, a strong default mix is:

- 5 H-1B layoff, transfer, or grace-period stories
- 3 sponsor-search or sponsor-employer stories
- 1 dependent/spouse impact story tied to an H-1B layoff or sponsor-search decision
- 1 high-signal adjacent story that directly improves AI expert answers or SEO for the H-1B layoff/sponsor-search journey

Adjust this mix when the user gives a specific campaign, source, or product goal.

Balance batches when possible across:

- India, China, and ROW
- H-1B, OPT/STEM OPT, and H-4/H-4 EAD where relevant to layoffs or sponsor search
- EB-1, EB-2, and EB-3 only when green card stage affects a layoff or sponsor-search decision
- early crisis, active filing, and outcome stages

Do not force balance by importing weak stories. Relevance, freshness, and outcome value come first.

## Query Themes By Source

The user must specify the source before collection. Once specified, these themes are appropriate starting points.

### Reddit

Useful communities may include H-1B, USCIS, immigration, F-1/OPT, cscareerquestions, or source-specific communities the user approves.

High-fit query themes:

- H-1B layoff grace period
- H-1B transfer premium processing
- H-1B sponsor search
- H-1B sponsoring companies
- H-1B transfer-friendly employer
- H-1B recruiter sponsorship
- H-1B RFE transfer
- H-1B to B-2 change of status
- H-1B to H-4 or H-4 EAD
- OPT layoff STEM OPT unemployment days
- Day 1 CPT after layoff
- I-140 approved job change
- PERM layoff restart
- EB-2/EB-3 priority date retrogression
- I-485 pending job change

### Rednote

High-fit query themes should focus on first-person U.S. immigration experiences from international students and workers.

Examples:

- H-1B layoff
- H-1B transfer
- 60-day grace period
- H-1B sponsor search
- H-1B sponsorship
- H-1B employer
- OPT unemployment
- Day 1 CPT
- H-4 EAD
- I-140 approved
- PERM timeline
- I-485 timeline
- priority date retrogression

For Rednote and other non-English sources, preserve the private source payload and generate the public draft in English only for now.

For RedNote stories where comments are not available from the collector, do not disqualify the story solely for lacking comments. Instead, require that the story itself contains a clear outcome or at least one concrete recommendation or idea from the poster for dealing with the situation. Open-ended questions with no proposed path forward should be skipped.

## Source Learning

Approved and prohibited source communities are not fixed yet. When exploring a source, learn which communities, keywords, or domains produce high-quality stories and record batch notes.

Until a source allowlist or blocklist exists:

- Prefer public, topic-specific communities where first-person immigration experience is common.
- Avoid communities dominated by politics, harassment, low-context memes, reposts, ads, or generic legal advice.
- Record which communities and query themes produced selected stories.
- Record which communities and query themes produced low-quality candidates.
- Do not treat one successful batch as permanent approval for a community.

## Skip Rules

Skip a candidate if:

- It fails the outside-source policy.
- It scores below 13.
- It has no outcome and no recommendations or ideas from the poster or commenters.
- It is not clearly connected to Haven's target audience.
- It is outside the 30-day freshness window without explicit approval.
- It is family-based, asylum, citizenship, or humanitarian content during the current operating period.
- It is mostly legal advice, not lived experience.
- It cannot be safely anonymized.
- It would require copying source phrasing to remain useful.
- It mainly names and criticizes a person, employer, school, or law firm.
- It is too old to reflect current process unless it has evergreen decision value.
- It would create confusion between community experience and verified legal guidance.

## Batch Review Notes

Every reviewed batch should include a short note with:

- Source used
- Query/theme used
- Freshness window used
- Number of candidates reviewed
- Number selected
- Number skipped
- Main skip reasons
- Intended product/story mix
- Audience balance notes
- Outcome vs unresolved-question count
- Base score and prioritization signal score for each selected story
- Meaningful comment value score for each selected story
- Summary of the best comments by category: real suggestions, previous experiences, proven expertise
- Borderline candidates worth showing to Haven before tightening criteria
- Any gaps the batch did not cover

## Cross-Batch Deduplication

Before importing a new batch, check every story against stories already imported in previous batches. A story is a duplicate if any of these match:

- Same `source_story_id` (case-insensitive)
- Same `source_url`
- Same `body_hash` (SHA-256 of the normalized body text)

When a duplicate is found:

- Skip it. Do not re-import or re-publish.
- Log the duplicate in the batch review notes with the original batch name and import run ID.
- If the duplicate appeared in an older batch that lacked rubric scores or case briefs, do not re-import it as a "refresh." Instead, update the original `community_import_items` row in place if better data is available.

The import script must query `community_import_items` before upserting to detect cross-batch duplicates by `source_story_id`, `source_url`, or `body_hash` in the `observability_metadata` field.
